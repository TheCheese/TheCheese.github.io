Ants

Although ants are a nuisance and a pest if they enter your home or set up their nests near your property, the fact is that human beings have been dealing with them for a long time. In fact, ants got here first, and we have been moving in on their territory ever since.

Ants come in all shapes, colours, sizes and temperaments. Some of them can beconsidered dangerous and aggressive, while others are considered useful and beneficial.

If you can find the nest it is possible to treat it by flooding the area with an approved pesticide however while this is sometimes effective it does make the assumption that there is only one colony and that the chemical will come into contact with the queen. It will sometimes appear that a treatment has been successful but if the queen is not destroyed the colony will reform.

The preferred method is the use of ant bait stations. These rely upon the ants carrying a slow acting poison back to the colony and eliminating the queen. There are both consumer and professional products that offer limited success in this regard but for an ant bait to work effectively it needs to tick a lot of boxes.

The majority of ant baits are formulated to only deal with sugar feeding ants however there are also protein and oil feeding species. It important to identify which species of ant you are dealing with and use the appropriate bait.

Having identified the correct species of any and choosing the correct type of bait you will get a result in many cases, however it is far from fool proof. Many ants can change from feeding on one type of food to another depending upon factors such as the life cycle of the young. This can also be compounded if there are multiple colonies requiring multiple feed types.

Some baits work too quickly and will either kill the workers before the queen has the opportunity to feed or will detect a problem and stop feeding the queen. Some baits are attractive to the workers but are rejected by the queen. If the queen is not killed she will continue to lay eggs with the result that the problem reoccurs some time later.
