Termite Control

Before choosing your Pest Control Company it is important to understand that under Victorian Legislation any pest control operator is legally allowed to perform termite work despite having had no studies or been tested in this area. This is contrary to the law in a number of other states who recognise that termites are a particularly difficult challenge and require the appropriate level of training prior to issuing a licence.

All of Raptor Pest Controls technicians have been formally trained in termite control and are fully insured for this application. Regardless of which company you ultimately decide to use we strongly advise you to do your homework in this area.

Termites have been around for more than 120 million years, dating back to the time of dinosaurs. While we think of them mostly as wood-eaters, they can also chew through other flooring and even wallpaper. They’re “silent invaders”, because you probably won’t hear or notice them until considerable damage has been done. In Australia termites are estimated to cause close to one billion dollars each year in property damage.

Prevention

Termites work quickly, causing devastating destruction to the least visible part of your home – the foundation. There are preventive steps you can take to keep termites away from your home, such as keeping wooden landscape features and mulch several inches below the wooden structure of your home.

Termites aid in the decaying process of dead trees, stumps, and roots, so promptly remove any decaying material or firewood from around the foundation of your home.

Termites love moisture. Make sure that:

  • Sprinklers and lawn irrigation are not directed at your foundation.
  • Gutter and rain control systems divert water away from the home.
  • Moisture is not penetrating your basement or crawlspace.

Termite Treatment

While we encourage you to take the precautions above, they are not a substitute for a professional termite control system.
Our exterminators understand the lifecycle and behaviours of termites and will know how to best search out and exterminate them. If damage or infestation is found, the pest control technician will know the best procedure for removing the termites and protecting your home in the future.

Modern post construction termite treatments offer a number of options including chemical trenching and bait stations located around the perimeter of the home.
There are pros and cons to each system and we will discuss these with you allowing you to make an informed decision as to which method you prefer.
